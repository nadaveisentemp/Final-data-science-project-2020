Background on NYC

In 1664, the British seized New Amsterdam from the Dutch and gave it a new name: New York City. For the next century, the population of New York City grew larger and more diverse. During the 18th century, NYC became a critical place for anti-british american colonial activity, but sadly, the city fell to the british a month after the united states of america declared their indpendence (august 1776) and served as an English millitary base until 1783. However, after the United States of America fully gained their independence, NYC slowly started evolving into the metropolis we know today, with it being having ports critical to the American economy in the 19th century, and in the 20th century, all five boroughs agreed to unite into one city, which at the time, they called a "Greater New York". However, the construction of new highways and suburbs after WW2 encouraged many people to leave the city, but then, the city became refilled with the diverse immigrant population we know today after the Hart-Cellar Immigration and Nationality Act of 1965. And today, it is a massive and well known five-borough metropolis that inhabits 8 million people (3.1 million of which are immigrants) and attracts 40 million tourists yearly.
